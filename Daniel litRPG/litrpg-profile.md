# LitRPG Recommendation Profile

I want to use you as an ongoing source for what to read or listen to next.

I am an avid LitRPG reader and audiobook listener. I want to share what I have read, what I liked and disliked, and where each **series** ranks for me. Judge by the series as it stands now, not book by book.

## What I want from you
- Build a profile of my taste from the notes below
- Recommend around **5 series**
- Tell me **why** I would like each one
- Prioritize **true LitRPG**
- Be honest when something is LitRPG-adjacent instead of true LitRPG
- Factor in **audiobook quality** when relevant

---

## My Taste Profile

### What I like
- True LitRPG elements
- Clear progression
- Levels, classes, skills, and concrete system mechanics
- Good writing
- Good pacing
- Strong characters
- Character growth
- Relationships that feel earned
- Humor
- Pop culture references in moderation
- Unique powers or systems
- Audiobooks with strong narration
- Real tension
- Stories where success is not guaranteed
- Completed stories, or series that clearly feel like they are going somewhere
- Memorable worlds and systems

### What I do not like
- Overpowered MCs with no real struggle
- Wish-fulfillment where the MC always wins easily
- Little or no growth
- Books that are not really LitRPG when I want LitRPG
- Excessive graphic sex that feels unnecessary
- Humor that drags on too long
- Indirect progression systems that do not scratch the leveling-up itch
- Long series that feel like a slog without enough payoff

## Key Series Notes

### Dungeon Crawler Carl
- One of my gold-standard series
- Loved the high stakes, chaotic banter, and Jeff Hays’s narration
- Strong benchmark for my taste

### The Perfect Run
- One of my favorite series
- Loved the unique power sets, humor, strong characters, and the fact that it is complete
- Even the bad guys felt well developed
- Main downside: no true level system

### The Ripple System / Shadeslinger
- Top 3 series for me
- Loved everything about it
- Especially liked the writing, pacing, characters, story, and relationship growth
- Very strong comparison point for future recommendations

### The Mayor of Noobtown
- I enjoy the world, system, humor, and pop culture references
- Main issue: the jokes sometimes go on too long

### Discount Dan
- I like the scrappy, comedic survival feel
- The "system" and items are S-tier for me, so creative and everyone will be unique.
- Already following new releases

### He Who Fights With Monsters
- I like the crunchy system
- I think it has some flaws, but it still lands well enough for me

### Ready Player One
- Solidly in my A-tier
- Relevant because I enjoy humor and pop culture when done well

### System Universe
- Dropped around Book 6

- There was not enough tension, struggle, or growth

### Mimic & Me

### Monster Farming (Kenny King)

### Chrysalis

### Apocaliptic Parenting

## Recommended (Session: 2026-04-11)

### 1. Defiance of the Fall — JF Brink (narrated by Travis Baldree)
- True LitRPG
- Closest to DCC in system depth and real stakes
- MC starts outmatched and earns every level
- Crunchy system, clear progression, top-tier Baldree narration
- Long ongoing series but each book moves the story forward
- Flag: MC does scale up, but threats scale with him

### 2. Primal Hunter — Zogarth
- True LitRPG
- Great system, genuine humor, real consequences
- DCC energy without copying it
- Ongoing but strong momentum and direction
- Flag: humor is more frequent than DCC

### 3. The Completionist Chronicles — Dakota Krout (narrated by Vikas Adam)
- True LitRPG
- Achievement-based system — creative and satisfying
- Vikas Adam narration is excellent
- Main series is complete
- Good humor and pop culture references
- Flag: MC gets strong, but threats scale to match

### 4. Azarinth Healer — Rhaegar
- True LitRPG
- Unique healer/berserker class combo
- Female MC, strong earned progression, real danger
- May have the "refreshing" quality noted with Apocalyptic Parenting
- Some adult content but not the focus
- Flag: early chapters are rough before writing hits its stride

### 5. The New World — Monsoon117
- True LitRPG
- Gritty, earned progression, genuinely dangerous system
- MC earns everything, strong early tension
- Unique skill-tree approach rather than class-based
- Flag: darker tone, less humor; audiobook narration is serviceable not standout
